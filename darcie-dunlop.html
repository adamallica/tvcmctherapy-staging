Darcie Dunlop graduated with honors from Pepperdine University and is a Licensed Marriage and Family Therapist with a master's degree in Marriage and Family Therapy. Prior to working in private practice, Darcie worked at Loma Linda University Behavioral Medicine Center, treating both adults and adolescents suffering from depression, anxiety, mood disorders, self-harm, and issues of communication.

She has specialized in working with alcohol and drug abuse, addiction, and recovery, as well as couples and families whose lives and relationships have been affected by addiction, codependency, betrayal, and/or the additional stress of raising a high-needs child. She is also experienced with adolescent and teen issues, and works with adults with high-functioning autism. She welcomes both straight folks and those in the LGBTQ+ community.

While no longer taking on new clients, Darcie now spends most of her time as Clinical Director — training the therapists at TVCMC to care for you, whoever you may be, in the same gentle way she has practiced throughout her career.

Groups

Darcie loves working with groups. She has been facilitating a Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT) group for the past eight years, focusing on Mindfulness, Interpersonal Skills, Emotional Regulation, and Distress Tolerance.

She also leads a Women's Mindfulness & Buddhist Psychology Group, using Buddhist Psychology to help create calm through the practice of mindfulness and meditation — making clear the path out of suffering into more effective ways of thinking and relating to ourselves, our families, our communities, and our world.

Both groups are open to new members. Please reach out to learn more about scheduling and availability.
