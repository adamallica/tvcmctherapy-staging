About Our Center

Rooted in compassion, guided by science, committed to your well-being.

Our Mission

At Temecula Valley Counseling and Mindfulness Center, our mission is to provide a warm, welcoming, and professionally excellent space where every person who walks through our doors feels safe, respected, and genuinely cared for.

"We believe healing is not a destination but a journey — and we are honored to walk alongside you every step of the way."

We serve individuals, couples, families, and groups across the Temecula Valley, offering therapy that honors the whole person — mind, body, and spirit.

Our therapists are trained in a variety of evidence-based modalities and approach each client with cultural humility, genuine curiosity, and deep respect for their unique experiences and goals.

Our Therapeutic Approach

  • Mindfulness-Based We weave mindfulness practices throughout our work to help clients develop present-moment awareness and self-compassion.
  • Evidence-Based Our therapists utilize approaches including CBT, DBT, EMDR, ACT, and more — tailored to each client's needs.
  • Culturally Sensitive We honor the diversity of our community and strive to provide culturally responsive, inclusive care.
  • Strengths-Focused We help clients identify and build upon their inherent strengths and resilience.
  • Collaborative Therapy is a partnership. We work with you, not for you, to define your goals and path forward.

Community & Connection

Our Groups

Facilitated by Darcie Dunlop, LMFT — both groups are open to new members.

Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT) Group

An ongoing group focused on four core skills: Mindfulness, Interpersonal Effectiveness, Emotional Regulation, and Distress Tolerance. Darcie has been facilitating this group for over eight years. Open to new members.

Women's Mindfulness & Buddhist Psychology Group

Using Buddhist Psychology to create calm through mindfulness and meditation — making clear the path out of suffering into more effective ways of thinking and relating to ourselves, our families, our communities, and our world. Open to new members.

We Can Never Be Strangers — Danna Faulds

Peace is This Moment — Dorothy Hunt

Do you think peace will come some other place than here? Some other time than now? In some other heart than yours? Peace is this moment without judgment. That is all. This moment in the heart-space where everything that is, is welcome. Peace is this moment without thinking that it should be some other way, that you should feel some other thing, that your life should unfold according to your plans. Peace is this moment without judgment, this moment in the heart-space where everything that is, is welcome.
